Human Slope Angle Perception in Virtual and Real Mountain Environments

A neuroscience research study

So what is this study?

This research study is being conducted by a professor of neuroscience at Westminster College in Salt Lake City, Utah. The purpose of this study is to evaluate where our human biases lie when it comes to perceiving the angles of snowy slopes in the backcountry. It will be conducted over the course of the 2021-22 ski season, with hopes of having results to present at the 2022 International Snow Science Workshop in Bend, OR.

This is one of the first online-only neuroscience research studies regarding this topic, developed in conjunction with computer science and communication undergraduate students at Westminster to develop the web application.

The ninety images were all taken in the Wasatch mountain range, by members of the team.

What is going to happen with my results?

Your results will be compiled with all the other survey results to create a complete analysis of what biases there are in how humans perceive snow slope angles in the backcountry. Your results will not be linked to your email or any personal information, and will be utilized for solely scientific purposes.
